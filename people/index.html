Since March 2025 I am a Postdoctoral Researcher in the MODUS team at GIPSA-lab (Grenoble, France) working with Bogdan Robu and Mtteo Tacchi. I am working inside the ANR project RADYAL with the objective to add `control’ to software and hardware optimization in order to reduce the energy consumption of neural networks.

I was a Postdoctoral Researcher for two months in the MAC team at LAAS–CNRS (Toulouse, France) working with Sophie Tarbouriech and Samuele Zoboli. I am working on coding LMIs in Python using cvxpy to insert an ETM in a neural network.

I completed my Ph.D. in the MAC team at LAAS–CNRS under the supervision of Isabelle Queinnec and Luca Zaccarian. My thesis topic was revolving around the control of resonant converters. You can find an under-construction website here. The experimental part of this work is done in collaboration with Carlos Olalla and Luis Martínez-Salamero at the Power Electronics laboratory of the GAEI group of Universitat Rovira i Virgili (URV) in Tarragona, Spain. During the thesis, I entered also in an ongoing work about the synchronization of identical linear multiagent systems in collaboration with Giulia Giordano and Sophie Tarbouriech.

I received the master’s degree in Mechatronics Engineering from the University of Trento, Italy, in 2021, following the bachelor’s degree in Industrial Engineering in 2018 in the Department of Industrial Engineering. It was during my master thesis where I contact Luca and proposed to me to collaborate on a project on resonant converter in Spain. Before that, I was enrolled at I.T.I.S. Rossi (Istituto Tecnico Industriale Statale in Italian) in Vicenza – a technical public high school – with the specialization in Electric-Electronics-Automation. I like to mention this since it is where all my passion for electronics and DIY burst out, and I had the possibility and time to submerge myself into it.

My main research interests include everything that is related to (industrial) engineering applications. To name a few: mechatronics, power electronics, electronics, robotics, … I like to tackle challenging problems from different point of views, test solutions on real prototype to go beyond simulation, and dive into implementation’s problems (which are always there to wait for us).
